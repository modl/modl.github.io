F2

F2 is an open and free web integration framework designed to help the financial services community develop custom web and mobile applications. F2 was the first project to graduate from the MOD/L process, and continues to be managed by the MOD/L team on GitHub. To date in 2013, we have released almost a dozen versions of F2, providing additional documentation and bolstering functionality. MOD/L continues receive feedback from the F2 community, and those recommendations are shaping the F2 roadmap.

Innovation Methodology

Do What You Want Day & Jetpack

In 2011, MOD started doing something we call Do What You Want Day (DWYWD), inspired by other organizations’ hackathons, ShipIt Days and even Google’s own 20% time. The basic idea: work on whatever you want for a day. We’ve now run 5 of these events in our Boulder offices, and several others in our Dallas, New York, and Noida offices, to great success.

Jetpack is a program to help any MOD employee take an idea from napkin sketch to full production implementation for a client in as short a time as possible. It arose out of our employees asking what to do with the really good DWYWD projects after they’re over, as well as our team’s passion to take great ideas, and make them real.

The Jetpack process itself is quite simple:

  • Make It Work (αlpha)
  • Make Sure It’s A Good Idea(βeta)
  • ! Live

The framework is intended to provide visibility, support and feedback, encourage lateral skills development, and ultimately, launch the best ideas MOD has to offer. Correspondingly, it outlines a means to stop an idea when it is not viable or is failing for any number of reasons, and it is the intent of this framework to make it safe for ideas and projects in this environment to fail; it is also the intent to help ensure success for projects that are going well.
